Potassium Ferrocyanide (K4[Fe(CN)6])

Essential Safety & Usage Information

Chemical Name: Potassium Ferrocyanide
Formula: K4[Fe(CN)6]
pH Level: ~6
6
Hazard Status:
Generally Safe (Handle with care)
Can Touch with Bare Hands?
✔ Yes – But avoid prolonged contact
Carcinogenic (Cancer Causing)?
❌ No – Not classified as carcinogenic
3D Molecular Structure:
Common Uses:
  • Used in blueprint paper and photography
  • Precipitation of heavy metals in wastewater treatment
  • Food additive (E536) to prevent caking in salt
  • Textile dyeing and electroplating
Safety Instructions:
  • Use gloves when handling in large quantities
  • Store in a dry, well-ventilated place
  • Do not mix with strong acids (releases toxic HCN gas)
  • Keep away from food and beverages
Interesting Fact: Despite its name, potassium ferrocyanide is not highly toxic because the cyanide groups are tightly bound to iron and do not release free cyanide under normal conditions.
